The science behind it

Why paper beats a screen

Physical puzzles engage the brain differently. Here's what the research says.

🧠
Cognitive Development
Pattern recognition and strategic thinking build executive function — the mental skill set behind planning, focus, and self-control.
→ Better problem-solving
📚
Literacy & Vocabulary
Visual scanning and repeated word exposure in themed word searches build spelling, reading readiness, and vocabulary naturally.
→ Stronger early literacy
✏️
Fine Motor Skills
Holding a pencil, circling letters, and writing numbers on paper develops the hand-eye coordination kids need for school.
→ Better handwriting
💪
Resilience & Patience
Unlike apps that give instant hints, paper puzzles teach kids to sit with difficulty — building the "try again" mindset that predicts long-term success.
→ Growth mindset
😴
Better Sleep
No blue light, no notifications, no overstimulation. Paper puzzles are a calming wind-down activity that support healthy sleep routines.
→ Reduced screen fatigue
